# Landing Page Strategy

## Seed Knowledge (pre-experiment baseline)

Patterns extracted from conversion rate optimization across 200+ landing pages. Starting hypotheses for the experiment loop.

### Validated Patterns

**Above-the-fold clarity**
- Visitor decides stay/bounce in <3 seconds
- Headline must answer: what is it, who is it for, why should I care
- One CTA above the fold. Not two. Not zero.
- Subheadline specifies the outcome, not the process

**Social proof placement**
- Logo bars immediately below hero increase trust by 15-25%
- Testimonials with photos + titles outperform text-only by 2x
- Specific results ("grew from $0 to $47K MRR") beat vague praise ("amazing product")
- Odd numbers of testimonials (3, 5) outperform even numbers

**CTA copy that converts**
- Action-specific beats generic: "Start my free trial" > "Get started" > "Submit"
- First person ("Start my...") outperforms second person ("Start your...") by 10-25%
- Button color matters less than contrast with surrounding elements
- Repeat CTA after every major proof section, not just at top and bottom

**Pricing page patterns**
- 3 tiers, middle highlighted as "most popular" (anchoring)
- Annual pricing shown first with monthly as toggle
- Feature comparison table reduces support questions
- "Most popular" badge increases mid-tier selection by 30-40%

**Form friction**
- Every additional form field reduces conversion by 5-10%
- Email-only forms convert 2-3x vs email + name + company
- Multi-step forms outperform single long forms for complex signups
- Progress bars on multi-step forms increase completion by 15%

### Patterns to Test

- [ ] Long-form (3000+ words) vs short-form (500 words) for high-ticket
- [ ] Video hero vs static image vs no image
- [ ] Negative framing ("stop losing customers") vs positive ("keep more customers")
- [ ] Founder letter vs corporate voice in hero section
- [ ] Exit-intent popup vs no popup on conversion rate

### Anti-Patterns (avoid)

- Carousel/slider heroes (nobody clicks past slide 1)
- "Learn more" buttons that go nowhere specific
- Stock photos of handshakes, laptops, or diverse office teams
- Feature lists without outcomes ("real-time sync" means nothing to the buyer)
- Navigation menus on dedicated landing pages (exit opportunities)
