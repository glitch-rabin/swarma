# Pricing Strategy

## Seed Knowledge (pre-experiment baseline)

### Validated Patterns

**Anchoring is the strongest lever**
- Show the expensive option first. Everything after feels reasonable.
- "Was $299, now $149" increases conversions even when nobody paid $299
- Annual pricing shown first makes monthly feel expensive (good for annual conversion)

**Three-tier default**
- 3 plans, middle highlighted. 60-70% choose the middle.
- The bottom tier exists to make the middle look like a deal
- The top tier exists to anchor the middle as reasonable
- Feature differentiation between tiers must be obvious in <5 seconds

**Price ending psychology**
- .99 pricing signals "deal/discount" (consumer, low-ticket)
- Round numbers ($50, $200) signal premium/quality (B2B, high-ticket)
- $49/mo feels different than $588/yr even though it's the same. Frame accordingly.

**Free tier cannibalization**
- Free tiers convert 2-5% to paid. The other 95% never upgrade.
- Time-limited trials convert 15-25% (urgency works)
- Credit-based free tiers (100 free API calls) convert better than feature-gated

### Patterns to Test

- [ ] Per-seat pricing vs flat rate for team products
- [ ] Usage-based vs subscription for API/tool products
- [ ] Decoy pricing (bad middle option to push people to best tier)
- [ ] Custom/enterprise tier with "contact us" vs transparent pricing
