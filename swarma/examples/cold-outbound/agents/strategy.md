# Cold Outbound Strategy

## Seed Knowledge (pre-experiment baseline)

Patterns from B2B outbound campaigns across SaaS, agencies, and consulting. Cold email/DM is a numbers game, but the right patterns compress the funnel.

### Validated Patterns

**Subject line rules**
- 4-7 words outperform longer subjects on open rate
- Lowercase, no punctuation reads as personal (not marketing)
- Name + specific reference: "re: your {recent_event}" opens at 40-60%
- Questions in subject line: effective only if genuinely relevant ("scaling the team?")
- Avoid: all caps, emojis, "quick question", "following up", "touching base"

**First line personalization depth**
- Generic: "I saw your company does X" -- 2% reply rate
- Signal-based: "Congrats on the Series B" -- 5% reply rate
- Insight-based: "Your job post for a growth lead suggests you're scaling acquisition" -- 12% reply rate
- The deeper the inference, the higher the reply rate. But: diminishing returns past 2 sentences of personalization.

**Email structure that converts**
- 3-5 sentences total. Nobody reads long cold emails.
- Line 1: personalized observation (not about you)
- Line 2: problem statement (pain they recognize)
- Line 3: what you do (one sentence, no jargon)
- Line 4: social proof (one specific result)
- Line 5: low-friction CTA ("worth a 15-min call?" not "book a demo")

**Follow-up cadence**
- Email 1: day 0 (initial outreach)
- Email 2: day 3 (different angle, same person)
- Email 3: day 7 (social proof, case study)
- Email 4: day 14 (breakup email: "closing the loop")
- 80% of replies come from follow-ups, not the first email
- After 4 touches, stop. More than 4 is spam.

**Channel mixing**
- LinkedIn connection request + email same day: 2x reply rate vs email alone
- LinkedIn voice note: 3x reply rate vs text DM (novelty effect, decaying)
- Twitter/X reply on their content before cold email: 40% higher open rate
- Sequence: social touch -> email -> social touch -> email

### Patterns to Test

- [ ] Video prospecting (Loom) vs text-only email
- [ ] Direct ask ("can we talk?") vs value-first ("here's something useful")
- [ ] Single decision-maker vs multi-thread (email CEO + director)
- [ ] Warm intro request vs cold outreach on same prospect list
- [ ] Negative CTA ("feel free to ignore this") vs positive CTA

### Anti-Patterns (avoid)

- "I hope this email finds you well" (instant delete)
- Attachments in cold emails (spam filters, trust barrier)
- Multiple CTAs in one email (decision paralysis)
- Scheduling links in first email (too presumptuous)
- Sending from a free email domain (credibility zero)
