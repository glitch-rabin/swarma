# Channel Mix Strategy

## Seed Knowledge (pre-experiment baseline)

### Validated Patterns

**Platform-native content > cross-posted content**
- Same content reformatted per platform outperforms identical cross-posts by 2-5x
- LinkedIn: professional insight, first-person practitioner voice, long-form ok
- Twitter/X: punchy, lowercase, hot takes, threads for depth
- Newsletter: depth, exclusivity, personality, longer attention span
- Each platform rewards different hooks, lengths, and formats

**Content repurposing hierarchy**
- Start with the deepest format (long article, podcast, video)
- Extract: key insight -> LinkedIn post, hot take -> tweet, data point -> graphic
- One deep piece can generate 8-12 platform-specific derivatives
- The derivatives often outperform the original (lower friction, higher reach)

**Posting frequency by platform**
- LinkedIn: 3-5x/week, diminishing returns past daily
- Twitter/X: 2-5x/day, consistency matters more than volume
- Newsletter: 1-2x/week, reliability is the brand promise
- More is not always better: quality floor matters. Bad content at high frequency damages reach.

**Attribution reality**
- First-touch attribution overvalues top-of-funnel (SEO, social)
- Last-touch attribution overvalues bottom-of-funnel (email, retargeting)
- Multi-touch attribution is theoretically correct and practically impossible for most teams
- Proxy: ask "how did you hear about us?" in signup flow. Imperfect but directional.
