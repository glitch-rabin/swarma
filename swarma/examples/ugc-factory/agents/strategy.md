# UGC Factory Strategy

## Seed Knowledge (pre-experiment baseline)

Data from 2026 growth ops landscape research. AI UGC is a cost arbitrage play -- the question is not whether AI can produce UGC, but how close to human performance you can get at 90-98% cost reduction.

### Cost Comparison (2026)

| Source | Cost Per Video | Notes |
|--------|---------------|-------|
| Human UGC creator | $150-2,000 | Varies by follower count, production quality |
| Arcads | $5-15 | Most lifelike AI actors |
| Creatify | $2-8 | Highest volume, lowest cost. URL-to-video. |
| MakeUGC | $3-10 | Fast (2-10 min processing) |
| ClipMake | $3-10 | Fastest brief-to-running-ad |
| LTX Studio | $5-15 | Multi-language recreation |

That's 90-98% cost reduction. At $5/video average, a $500 budget produces 100 variants. A human creator budget of $500 produces 1-3 videos.

### Performance Data (2026)

- AI UGC performs within **10-20% of human creator content** on ROAS for short-form ads under 60 seconds
- MakeUGC: **3.1x average ROAS** across brand portfolio
- Arcads case studies: **$130K+/month revenue** through AI UGC campaigns
- Best practice: rotate new UGC every **2-3 weeks** for sustained ROAS
- Hook rate (3-second view rate) is the single most predictive metric for ad performance

### Authentic Voice Patterns

UGC that converts sounds like a real person talking to a friend:
- Sentence fragments. Incomplete thoughts. Natural pauses.
- "I was literally about to..." > "I was considering..."
- "This thing is insane" > "This product is exceptional"
- Problem-first: always start with the pain before the solution
- No marketing language. No superlatives. No "revolutionary."
- Filler words (actually, honestly, literally) increase perceived authenticity

### Script Archetypes (ranked by average CPA)

1. **Problem-solution**: "I used to struggle with X. Then I found Y." -- most reliable CPA
2. **Before-after**: "Here's my [metric] before... and here's after 30 days." -- best for visual products
3. **Testimonial**: "I've been using X for 3 months now and honestly..." -- best for warm audiences
4. **Unboxing reaction**: "Okay so this just arrived and..." -- best for physical products
5. **"I found this thing"**: "So my friend told me about this and I had to try it." -- best for cold audiences

### A/B Hook Testing Protocol

The hook is 80% of the video's success. For every script body, generate 20-50 different hooks:
- Different opening lines
- Different emotional angles (excitement, frustration, curiosity, shock)
- Different specificity levels ("this $12 tool" vs "this tool" vs "this skincare tool")
- Different direct address ("you need to see this" vs "nobody's talking about this" vs "I can't believe this works")

Launch all hooks as separate ad sets. 48 hours of data. Kill bottom 80%. Scale the winning hook.

### Platform Compliance (2026)

- **EU AI Act**: Mandatory labeling of AI-generated content. Synthetic media disclosure required on all AI avatar videos. Non-compliance = fines.
- **Meta**: Currently allows AI-generated ads but requires "Made with AI" label on AI-generated/modified images.
- **TikTok**: No specific AI label requirement yet, but trending toward disclosure.

Build disclosure into the pipeline from day one. Don't retrofit later.

### Refresh Cadence

- Week 1-2: peak performance. Let winners run.
- Week 2-3: performance plateau. Prepare next batch.
- Week 3-4: fatigue onset (CTR drops >20%). Swap in new creative.
- Monthly: full creative refresh. New scripts, new hooks, new avatars.
- Quarterly: new script archetypes based on cumulative performance data.

### Patterns to Test

- [ ] AI avatar vs human creator on identical script and audience
- [ ] Hook volume: 10 vs 50 variants per script body
- [ ] Avatar-audience demographic match vs mismatch
- [ ] Script archetype ranking per product category
- [ ] Refresh cadence: 2 weeks vs 3 weeks vs 4 weeks
- [ ] Multi-language dubbing of top performers

### Anti-Patterns

- Using the same AI avatar for all ads (audience recognizes = uncanny valley)
- Polished/corporate tone in UGC format (defeats the purpose)
- Skipping EU AI Act disclosure (legal risk, not worth it)
- Testing fewer than 20 hook variants (not enough to find a statistical winner)
- Running the same creative past 4 weeks (guaranteed fatigue)
