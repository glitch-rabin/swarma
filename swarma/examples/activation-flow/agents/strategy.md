# Activation Strategy

## Seed Knowledge (pre-experiment baseline)

Patterns from PLG (product-led growth) companies. The gap between signup and first value moment is where most users churn. These are starting hypotheses.

### Validated Patterns

**Time-to-value is everything**
- Users who reach "aha moment" in first session retain at 3-5x the rate of those who don't
- The aha moment is product-specific: Slack = first team message, Dropbox = first file sync
- Every step between signup and aha is a dropout point. Minimize steps ruthlessly.

**Onboarding checklist pattern**
- Checklists with 3-5 items and visible progress increase activation by 20-30%
- Start the checklist pre-filled (1 of 5 done) -- zero progress feels demotivating
- Each item should deliver visible value, not just collect information
- Completion triggers should be celebrated (micro-animations, congrats)

**Empty state design**
- Empty dashboards kill activation. Never show a blank screen.
- Pre-populate with sample data, templates, or guided actions
- "Create your first X" is more effective than a feature tour
- The empty state IS the onboarding

**Email sequence timing**
- Welcome email within 5 minutes of signup (not 1 hour, not next day)
- Day 1: value-first (show them what they can do, not what you do)
- Day 3: social proof (show them who else uses it and what they achieved)
- Day 7: urgency (trial ending, limited offer, or "we noticed you haven't...")
- Beyond day 7: diminishing returns. If they haven't activated, the product failed them.

**Segmented onboarding**
- Ask ONE question at signup: "What's your main goal?"
- Route to different onboarding flows based on answer
- Generic onboarding converts 15-20% worse than goal-specific flows
- 2-3 segments is enough. More than 5 creates maintenance burden.

### Patterns to Test

- [ ] Interactive tutorial vs video walkthrough vs text guide
- [ ] Human welcome message (from founder) vs automated system message
- [ ] Onboarding call offer (high-touch) vs pure self-serve for conversion rate
- [ ] Progressive disclosure (show features gradually) vs full access from day 1
- [ ] Gamification (points, badges) vs straightforward task completion

### Anti-Patterns (avoid)

- Feature tours that show every feature before user does anything
- Mandatory profile completion before reaching the product
- "Skip" buttons on critical onboarding steps (users always skip)
- Tooltips that appear once and are never seen again
- Login required before showing any product value
