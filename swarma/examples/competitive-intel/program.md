# Competitive Intel

## Mission

Monitor competitive landscape and surface actionable signals. Not a news feed -- a decision support system that tells you what matters and what to do about it.

## How Growth Teams Actually Do This

Competitive intelligence in growth teams is not "track competitors." It's:

- **Signal over noise**: 95% of competitor activity doesn't matter. identify the 5% that does.
- **Pattern recognition**: one feature launch is noise. three feature launches in the same direction is a strategic signal.
- **So-what analysis**: "competitor launched X" is not intelligence. "competitor launched X, which means they're betting on Y, which threatens our Z" is intelligence.
- **Speed**: the value of intel decays fast. a weekly digest is too slow for tactical decisions.

## Experiment Patterns

1. **Signal quality** -- daily scan vs weekly deep dive. which surfaces more actionable insights per hour spent?
2. **Source mix** -- product changelog + social + job postings + funding news. which sources predict strategic moves earliest?
3. **Alert threshold** -- report everything vs filter to high-impact only. what's the right noise-to-signal ratio?
4. **Brief format** -- bullet summary vs narrative analysis vs structured decision framework. which format drives the most action?
5. **Prediction testing** -- make predictions based on intel, track accuracy. calibrate over time.

## Metrics

- **Primary**: signal_actionability (self-eval on specificity + relevance + so-what clarity)
- **Production**: briefs_that_drove_decisions, prediction_accuracy, response_time_to_threats via manual scoring
- **Signal**: high volume of alerts + low action rate = too much noise. low volume + high action rate = good calibration.

## Constraints

- Every brief must answer "so what?" and "what should we do?"
- No opinion without evidence. "they might be pivoting" requires 2+ data points.
- Focus on leading indicators (job postings, API changes, pricing moves) not lagging (press releases, blog posts).
- Distinguish between tactical (respond this week) and strategic (affects our roadmap) signals.
