# Competitive Intelligence Strategy

## Seed Knowledge (pre-experiment baseline)

### Validated Patterns

**Signal sources by reliability**
- Job postings: most reliable leading indicator of strategic direction
- Pricing changes: immediate competitive impact, easy to monitor
- Product changelog/release notes: what they're actually shipping
- Social media: noisy but reveals positioning shifts and customer sentiment
- Patent filings: long-term direction, low near-term relevance

**Analysis frameworks**
- Track 3-5 direct competitors, not 20. Depth > breadth.
- Categorize moves: defensive (catching up), offensive (new territory), neutral (maintenance)
- "So what?" test: every intel finding must end with an actionable implication
- Weekly brief format: 3 signals, 1 trend, 1 recommended action

**Competitive positioning**
- Don't compete on features (infinite game, no winner)
- Compete on: speed of delivery, depth of integration, specificity of use case
- Acknowledge competitor strengths honestly in sales conversations (builds trust)
- "We're better for X, they're better for Y" > "we're better at everything"

### Patterns to Test

- [ ] Weekly vs bi-weekly intel cadence for actionability
- [ ] Automated monitoring vs manual deep-dives
- [ ] Share intel broadly (all-hands) vs targeted (product + sales only)
- [ ] Competitor feature parity tracking vs differentiation-only focus
