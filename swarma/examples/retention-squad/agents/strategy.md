# Retention Strategy

## Seed Knowledge (pre-experiment baseline)

### Validated Patterns

**Churn signals appear before churn**
- Usage frequency drop (3+ days of inactivity for daily-use products)
- Feature adoption plateau (stopped exploring new features)
- Support ticket escalation (frustration accumulating)
- Payment method expiring (involuntary churn, preventable)

**Reactivation windows**
- Day 3-7 after last activity: highest reactivation rate (40-60% with right nudge)
- Day 14-30: moderate (15-25%), requires stronger incentive
- Day 30+: near-zero without significant product change or offer
- "We miss you" emails work once. After that, show product value.

**Retention levers by stage**
- Week 1: activation completion (did they reach aha moment?)
- Month 1: habit formation (did they build it into their workflow?)
- Month 3: expansion (are they using more features, inviting team?)
- Month 6+: lock-in (switching costs, data accumulation, integrations)

**Win-back patterns**
- Downgrade offer > cancellation. Keep them on any paid plan.
- Exit survey with real-time response (offer to fix the cited problem)
- "Pause instead of cancel" reduces permanent churn by 15-20%
- Personalized win-back based on their specific usage pattern > generic offer

### Patterns to Test

- [ ] Proactive outreach at first churn signal vs reactive after churn
- [ ] Feature spotlight emails vs usage milestone celebrations
- [ ] Community access (forum, Slack) as retention tool vs product-only
- [ ] Annual plan discount as retention lever vs month-to-month flexibility
