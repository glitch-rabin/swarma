# Referral Strategy

## Seed Knowledge (pre-experiment baseline)

### Validated Patterns

**Double-sided incentives outperform single-sided**
- "Give $20, get $20" (Dropbox, Uber) consistently outperforms "Refer a friend"
- The referrer needs a reason to feel good about sharing, not just a bribe
- Best referral programs benefit the referred person more than the referrer

**Timing the ask**
- Ask after a success moment (first win, milestone, positive feedback)
- Never ask during onboarding (too early, no loyalty yet)
- Best trigger: user just experienced measurable value (saved time, made money, got compliment)
- Post-NPS survey: promoters (9-10) get referral prompt immediately

**Friction kills referrals**
- Pre-written shareable message: 3x more shares than blank text box
- Unique referral link > code entry (nobody remembers codes)
- One-click sharing to specific platform > generic "share" button
- Mobile-first: most referrals happen on phones, not desktop

**Viral coefficient math**
- K-factor = invitations sent x conversion rate
- K > 1 = viral growth (rare, most products are 0.2-0.5)
- Focus on increasing invitation rate first, then conversion rate
- Network effects compound: each new user makes the product more valuable to existing users

### Patterns to Test

- [ ] Monetary incentive vs product credit vs feature unlock
- [ ] In-app referral prompt vs email campaign
- [ ] Milestone-triggered ask vs random/periodic ask
- [ ] Social proof ("47 people from your network use this") vs direct ask
