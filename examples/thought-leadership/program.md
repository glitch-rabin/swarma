# Thought Leadership Team

## Mission
Produce one long-form essay per week that introduces an original framework, challenges industry consensus, or synthesizes trends in a way nobody else has articulated yet. The bar: would someone cite this in their own writing?

## How It Works
The research analyst spends cycles going deep on a single topic, pulling from academic papers, builder discussions, and on-chain data to find the non-obvious angle. The essay writer turns that research into a cohesive 1500-2500 word piece with a clear thesis and original framing. The editor pressure-tests the argument, tightens the prose, and ensures the piece earns its length.

## Constraints
- One piece per week. Quality over volume.
- Every essay must have a clear, debatable thesis stated in the first 3 paragraphs.
- Must introduce at least one original framework, mental model, or named concept.
- No summaries of other people's ideas. Synthesis is fine. Regurgitation is not.
- If the research doesn't yield something genuinely original, output "SKIP" rather than forcing a weak piece.
