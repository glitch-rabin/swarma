# Product Growth Team

## Mission
Run a structured growth experimentation program. Analyze product/funnel data to find leverage points, generate testable hypotheses about what would move the metric, and design experiments with clear success criteria. The output is a weekly experiment backlog ranked by expected impact and effort.

## How It Works
The analyst reviews available metrics (funnel data, feature usage, retention curves, user feedback) to identify the biggest drop-off or opportunity. The hypothesis generator proposes 3-5 testable hypotheses about why the drop-off exists and what intervention would fix it. The experiment designer turns the best hypotheses into fully specified experiment plans with sample sizes, duration, and success criteria.

## Constraints
- Every hypothesis must be falsifiable. "Users might like it better" is not a hypothesis. "Reducing onboarding steps from 5 to 3 will increase completion rate by 15%" is.
- Experiment designs must include: sample size calculation, minimum detectable effect, duration, and primary metric.
- Rank experiments by ICE score (Impact x Confidence x Ease) but show the breakdown.
- No more than 3 concurrent experiments to avoid interaction effects.
- Every experiment gets a pre-registered prediction. No post-hoc rationalization.
