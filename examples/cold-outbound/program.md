# Cold Outbound

## Mission

Optimize cold outreach messaging through systematic experimentation. Find the angles, formats, and personalization depth that turn cold contacts into warm conversations.

## How Growth Teams Actually Do This

Outbound is a numbers game with a quality multiplier. Most teams spray and pray. Growth teams test:

- **First-touch angle**: pain-point vs opportunity vs mutual connection vs relevant insight
- **Message length**: 2 sentences vs 5 sentences vs detailed pitch
- **Personalization depth**: name + company vs role-specific vs "I saw your post about X"
- **CTA type**: soft ask (question) vs hard ask (meeting) vs value-first (free resource)
- **Sequence cadence**: 3-day vs 7-day followups, 3-touch vs 5-touch sequences

The best outbound teams run these as experiments with sample sizes, not vibes.

## Experiment Patterns

1. **Pain vs opportunity** -- "struggling with X?" vs "teams like yours are doing X"
2. **Short vs long** -- 2-sentence cold open vs detailed value prop
3. **Personalization depth** -- generic role-based vs specific research-based
4. **Social proof placement** -- lead with credibility vs lead with insight
5. **CTA softness** -- "worth a chat?" vs "I have 15 min Tuesday" vs "here's a resource"
6. **Followup timing** -- 3-day vs 7-day gaps between touches

## Metrics

- **Primary**: reply_likelihood (self-eval on would-you-reply-to-this)
- **Production**: reply_rate, positive_reply_rate, meeting_booked_rate via webhook
- **Signal**: high reply rate + low meeting rate = message is interesting but CTA is wrong. fix the CTA, not the message.

## Constraints

- No spam patterns. No "I hope this email finds you well." No fake personalization.
- Every message must pass the "would I reply to this?" test.
- Personalization must be genuine -- if it reads like mail merge, it's wrong.
